The context

The governance gap is already open.

Staff across every department can now build apps, automations, scripts, and lightweight workflows with AI. The issue is not that this is happening. The issue is that most organisations do not yet have an operating model that makes it visible, governable, and safe at speed.

Why this matters after the survey

The risk check diagnoses where exposure is likely to sit. The Vibe Code Lab is the response model: a structured, IT-led environment that closes the gap between uncontrolled demand and governed delivery.

If visibility is weak The Lab creates a known route and a known environment.
If control is inconsistent The Lab introduces guardrails, review points, and ownership.
If risk is rising The Lab brings data, testing, and governance into the build path.
If strategy is lagging The Lab gives IT a practical operating model it can lead with.

How it works

Four phases. One coherent framework.

Phase 01 — Discover

Discover

Using Tactica’s AS-IS / TO-BE methodology, engage stakeholders to understand business AI strategy, surface the frustrations driving demand, and build a fact-based view of what is currently in use or development — what is working, what is ungoverned, where the gaps are, and where current or emerging risks sit.

Phase 02 — Design

Design

Define the target operating model for AI-enabled development: approved tool stack, architectural principles, review paths, control points, and the route by which ideas move from experimentation to controlled MVP and onward into real business use.

Phase 03 — Govern

Govern

Establish the guardrails, review gates, decision rights, and accountability structures that make the model safe and repeatable. This is not about slowing the business down. It is about making decisions visible, consistent, and defensible.

Phase 04 — Enable

Enable

Stand up the Vibe Code Lab environment and operating rhythm. Give teams a governed space to build with AI — with visibility, data control, testing, and progression into business use all built in from the start.
