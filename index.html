Not assumed.   Not hoped.   Actually known.
What's happening

Something is already happening

Across most organisations, people are already using AI tools to build automations, workflows, and applications — often without formal IT visibility, often in hours, and sometimes already in business use before governance has seen them.

And it is not just development. AI-generated content is circulating in internal and external communications without review, approval, or any organisational awareness that it is happening.

The practical reality

This activity usually does not begin as a formal programme. It begins as teams solving problems locally, moving quickly, and using the tools available to them.

It is not a technology issue. It is an operating model issue — and most organisations have not addressed it yet.

Automations

Workflow logic, approvals, triggers, and task orchestration built rapidly with AI support — often outside IT's line of sight.

Applications

Internal tools and lightweight apps built outside the usual delivery path and moved into business use quickly.

Content creation

AI-generated content circulating in emails, reports, and communications — internally and externally — without review, approval, or organisational awareness.

Business reliance

Outputs already being relied upon before ownership, assurance, and governance are fully defined.

The three positions

Where do you sit?

Most organisations land in one of three places when they look honestly at the picture.

Controlled

Controlled

Visibility exists, guardrails are in place, and there is a credible strategy. The work here is about formalising what is working before fragmented behaviour creates new gaps.

Exposed

Exposed

AI-enabled activity is moving into business use faster than IT can govern it. Tools are being built and relied upon before visibility, ownership, and control have caught up.

Blindspot

Blindspot

The organisation does not yet have a reliable view of what AI-enabled development activity exists. The most serious issue is not what is known, but what remains unseen.

The real issue

AI-assisted development is already happening

The question is whether it happens through IT or around IT.

Through IT Visible, governed, and aligned
or
Around IT Fast, local, and less controlled

In many organisations today, it is the latter.

What needs to change

This is not about slowing things down

It is about creating a credible route where the business can move quickly, IT retains visibility and control, and governance becomes part of delivery rather than something applied afterwards.
